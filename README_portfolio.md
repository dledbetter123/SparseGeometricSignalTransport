A multi-year applied-math research program asking why self-attention works — and whether a cheaper geometric object computes the same thing. The wager: a transformer block is really performing parallel transport of information along a learned connection on a fiber bundle, so attention's O(T²) all-pairs cost is one expensive way to realize that transport, not the only way.

The thesis turns on one observation. Attention is path-independent: the score between two tokens depends only on their content, never on what lies between them. Language is path-dependent — meaning accrues along the whole route through a clause. That is exactly the regime gauge theory describes through holonomy, the rotation a vector picks up when transported around a curved space. SGST builds tokens as sparse "constellations" of Fourier modes where shared modes are the connections, and moves information by a spectral Wilson line eᶦ⁽⁻ω∫A⁾ — the content-dependent phase rotation attention cannot express.

Honest results, because they are the point. Across twenty-one iterations the geometry never beat GPT outright — the closest parity came from conventional components, and the best legitimate spectral run landed within 1.6× of GPT on WikiText-103. The audit located the real constraint: not representation but a 128× state-capacity gap against attention's KV cache. But the program produced a transferable win — CurvBias, a drop-in positional encoding that adds learned curvature where RoPE is flat, improving perplexity ~9% over RoPE at matched scale while staying compatible with FlashAttention.

The forward case: a complex-valued, capacity-matched, sparse-spectral fiber bundle with selective (delta-rule) transport is the most promising sub-quadratic generative architecture in the design space six independent research lines — gauge theory, state-space models, the delta rule, spectral methods, optimal transport, geometric deep learning — all point toward. The geometric framing is what tells you which operations deserve the next order of magnitude of compute, instead of guessing.
